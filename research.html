FIG. 01 — Research

Research and development for bio-based materials, durability questions, and product validation.

R&D work centers on forming hypotheses, mapping variables, and designing tests that reveal how materials behave under real product constraints.

  1. 01

    Test plans

    Coating durability, hydrolysis, abrasion, and repairability, tracked across repeatable trial structures.

  2. 02

    Screening frameworks

    Bio-derived finishes, fiber blends, and end-of-life tradeoffs, compared against sourcing and durability constraints.

  3. 03

    Translation to product specs

    Turning lab findings into field-testing criteria that a product or design team can actually act on.

Automation

Automation here is not about replacing craft — it's about reducing friction in documentation, testing, comparison, and iteration, so product teams can learn faster. Repeatable comparison templates and simple scoring structures help teams compare claims, risks, and next tests.
